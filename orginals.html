PRETEND | Shashaa ‚ù§Ô∏è Ft. Keba (EP: I'm Sorry, Heart) | OFFICIAL VIDEO

ABOUT 'PRETEND':
In this make-believe relationship, the sentiment behind Pretend is analogous to a couple's fight - and what it entails - the expectations and extent of responsibility taken, degree of communication, speculation, scrutiny and ego clashes, mutual 'needs' of apology and owning up and the resulting emotional and physical rift that is often created in the process.
However, in this concocted relationship where nothing was real to begin with, the perceived, one-sided rift and grief is self-inflicted, the basis of it stemming from something as simple as a dwindling frequency of communication or a dying intensity in interaction, subsequently leaving one feeling insignificant, hollow and dispensible.
Keba's minimal guitars dovetailed with almost spoken vocals maintain the intimacy, emptiness and earthy austerity of an aching heart.

I'M SORRY, HEART | Shashaa ‚ù§Ô∏è Ft. Keba (EP: I'm Sorry, Heart) | OFFICIAL VIDEO

ABOUT 'I'M SORRY, HEART':
Ever feel that feeling where every inch of you is resisting a whirlwind someone's created in you? When you know nothing fruitful is to come of fuelling it? And yet you're sucked into the conundrum of right and wrong and caught in the tug-of-war between practicality and fantasy?
A vulnerable voice feebly bargains and justifies this unsolicited predicament with what it can't choose to hide itself from, ie, the heart. Which is the premise of the title track, 'I'm Sorry, Heart'.
There is an intentional reluctance and apprehensiveness with which Keba's guitars occasionally sheepishly embody and at times unabashedly steer the vocals, emanating the push and pull between reason and innocent petulance.

IN MY SKIN | Shashaa ‚ù§Ô∏è Ft. Keba (EP: I'm Sorry, Heart) | OFFICIAL VIDEO

ABOUT 'IN MY SKIN':
There's no limiting the passion and extent of intimacy that can be lived within the realms of the mind. 'In My Skin' shyly exclaims, with the help of seductively intertwining layers of vocal harmonies in tandem with unpredictable playfulness on the acoustic guitar, how even the masked intensity of sheer musing towards someone clueless, has the capacity to stir up what had grown dormant, immeasurably jolting the senses to life. Embracing THAT moment, logic aside, is the dogma of 'In My Skin'.
The momentum created by Keba's dynamically changing, pulsating, almost teasing patterns are synonymous to a racing heartbeat, etching towards a climactic pique at the onset of the Chorus and eventually settling down to a rather poignant calm.
